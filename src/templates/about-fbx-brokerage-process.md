# Florida Business Exchange Brokerage Process

## Overview

Florida Business Exchange (FBX) follows a structured process to help business owners successfully sell their companies.

Selling a business is a complex process that typically takes **6-12 months** from start to closing. The FBX process is designed to:

- maximize the sale price
- attract qualified buyers
- maintain confidentiality
- manage the transaction efficiently

FBX acts as the **lead advisor and transaction manager**, guiding both the seller and buyer through each stage of the process.

---

# Phase 1: Initial Consultation

## Objective

Understand the business and determine whether the owner is ready to begin the sale process.

## Activities

During the initial consultation, FBX gathers information about:

- the business model
- revenue and profitability
- ownership structure
- industry position
- growth opportunities
- the owner's goals for selling

The advisor also explains the typical sales process and answers questions about how business sales work.

## Outcome

At the end of this phase, both parties determine whether it makes sense to move forward with a formal valuation and engagement.

---

# Phase 2: Valuation and Engagement

## Objective

Determine the likely market value of the business and formally engage FBX as the advisor.

## Activities

FBX reviews financial documents including:

- profit and loss statements
- tax returns
- balance sheets
- owner compensation
- operational expenses

The advisor then performs a valuation analysis using common methods such as:

- Seller's Discretionary Earnings (SDE) multiples
- EBITDA multiples
- comparable transactions
- industry benchmarks

Once the valuation is completed, the owner reviews the expected valuation range and decides whether to proceed with the sale process.

## Outcome

If the owner decides to move forward, an **engagement agreement** is signed authorizing FBX to represent the business in the market.

---

# Phase 3: Preparing the Business for Market

## Objective

Prepare professional marketing materials and position the business to attract qualified buyers.

## Activities

FBX prepares documentation that may include:

- a Confidential Information Memorandum (CIM)
- financial summaries
- operational overviews
- growth opportunities
- buyer qualification requirements

This information helps buyers understand the business while protecting sensitive details.

Confidentiality is critical at this stage to ensure employees, customers, and competitors do not learn that the business is for sale.

## Outcome

The business is fully prepared to be introduced to qualified buyers.

---

# Phase 4: Buyer Identification and Outreach

## Objective

Identify and contact qualified buyers who may be interested in acquiring the business.

## Activities

FBX markets the business through several channels:

- internal buyer databases
- private equity relationships
- strategic buyers in the industry
- targeted outreach to qualified investors
- industry contacts

Interested buyers must complete a qualification process before receiving detailed information.

This typically includes:

- signing a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- demonstrating financial capability
- confirming acquisition interest

## Outcome

Qualified buyers gain access to detailed information about the business.

---

# Phase 5: Buyer Discussions and Management Meetings

## Objective

Allow serious buyers to learn more about the business and evaluate the opportunity.

## Activities

Qualified buyers may:

- review financial data
- ask operational questions
- meet with the owner and management team
- discuss transition plans

FBX manages communication between the buyer and seller to ensure the process remains organized and professional.

## Outcome

Interested buyers prepare formal offers.

---

# Phase 6: Offers and Letter of Intent (LOI)

## Objective

Select the best buyer and establish the key terms of the transaction.

## Activities

Buyers submit written offers, typically in the form of a **Letter of Intent (LOI)**.

The LOI outlines:

- purchase price
- deal structure
- financing terms
- closing timeline
- due diligence requirements

FBX helps the seller evaluate offers based on the full package, not just price.

Important considerations include:

- likelihood of closing
- buyer experience
- financing strength
- transition expectations

## Outcome

The seller accepts the best offer and signs the LOI.

---

# Phase 7: Due Diligence

## Objective

Allow the buyer to verify the financial and operational information about the business.

## Activities

The buyer reviews documentation such as:

- detailed financial records
- tax returns
- customer contracts
- vendor agreements
- employee information
- legal documents

FBX helps coordinate document requests and ensures the process remains organized.

Regular communication between the parties helps keep the transaction moving forward.

## Outcome

Once due diligence is successfully completed, the parties move toward final legal documentation.

---

# Phase 8: Closing the Transaction

## Objective

Finalize the legal agreements and transfer ownership of the business.

## Activities

Attorneys prepare the final transaction documents, which may include:

- asset purchase agreement or stock purchase agreement
- employment or transition agreements
- non-compete agreements
- financing documentation

FBX works with the attorneys, CPA, lenders, and other advisors to ensure all closing requirements are satisfied.

## Outcome

The transaction closes and ownership transfers to the buyer.

---

# Typical Timeline

The typical timeline for selling a business is:

Preparation: 4-8 weeks
Marketing and buyer outreach: 2-4 months
Negotiation and LOI: 1 month
Due diligence and closing: 2-3 months

Actual timelines vary depending on the complexity of the transaction.

---

# Role of Florida Business Exchange

FBX acts as the **transaction quarterback**, coordinating the entire process and keeping the deal moving forward.

The firm helps:

- prepare the business for sale
- identify qualified buyers
- manage negotiations
- coordinate due diligence
- work with attorneys and accountants
- ensure the transaction closes successfully

---

# Goal of the Process

The goal of the FBX process is to create a **competitive sale environment** where multiple buyers evaluate the business.

Competition between buyers often leads to:

- higher purchase prices
- stronger deal terms
- greater certainty of closing
